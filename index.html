Projects

Dual channel DC power supply

Third Semester Project

Designed and implemented a dual-channel linear DC power supply as part of the Electronic Devices & Circuits course at NUST CEME, including the entire workflow from simulation to PCB design and hardware testing. The DC power supply provides a variable voltage between 2V and 24V, along with a regulated 5V DC supply, with stable performance, including regulation of 5% and ripple of 10%, and efficiency of 73%. This project provided a real-world application of AC-DC conversion, voltage regulation, and thermal management.

Designed and implemented a fully functional 4-bit Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) for a Digital Logic Design project, translating fundamental logic gates into a complete computational unit.
